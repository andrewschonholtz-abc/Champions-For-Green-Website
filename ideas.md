## Approach 1: "Editorial Warmth" — Vuori-Meets-Magazine

**Design Movement:** New Editorial Minimalism — inspired by premium lifestyle magazines like Kinfolk and Cereal, combined with the warm, approachable energy of Vuori's digital presence.

**Core Principles:**
1. Warm restraint — generous whitespace with warm Linen canvas, never sterile
2. Typography-led hierarchy — headlines dominate, body copy whispers
3. Photographic immersion — images occupy 50-65% of every major section
4. Rhythmic alternation — light editorial sections alternate with dark cinematic sections

**Color Philosophy:** The Linen (#F8F3E8) canvas creates warmth and approachability that distinguishes CFG from cold corporate sustainability sites. Forest Green (#243D32) anchors authority. Gold (#C9A96E) adds premium accents sparingly — like gold foil on a luxury magazine cover. The palette says "we are serious about craft" without saying "we are an eco company."

**Layout Paradigm:** Asymmetric editorial grid. Hero sections use 60/40 splits. Product sections use offset grids where images bleed past their containers. Text blocks are narrow (max 680px) and left-aligned, creating a reading rail that guides the eye. Dark sections break the rhythm like chapter dividers in a book.

**Signature Elements:**
1. Gold tracked section labels (THE CURRENCY, THE DIFFERENCE) floating above headlines like magazine rubrics
2. Large monospace bottle count numbers that feel like data visualization, not decoration
3. Thin gold horizontal rules as section dividers

**Interaction Philosophy:** Subtle and purposeful. No flashy animations. Smooth scroll-triggered reveals. Hover states that feel like turning a page — slight lifts, color shifts, underline reveals. The single CTA button has a confident, quiet hover state.

**Animation:** Fade-up on scroll for text blocks (staggered 100ms). Image sections reveal with a subtle scale from 1.02 to 1.0. Bottle count numbers count up on scroll into view. Navigation has a clean backdrop blur on scroll. No parallax — movement is vertical and intentional.

**Typography System:** DM Sans Bold for headlines (44-56px hero, 28-36px sections) — geometric, modern, premium. DM Sans Regular for body (16-18px). Space Mono for data/stats/bottle counts. Section labels in DM Sans Bold, 12-14px, ALL-CAPS, 0.15em tracking, Gold color.

</idea>
<text>This approach channels the editorial warmth of Vuori and Kinfolk magazine, using asymmetric layouts, warm Linen backgrounds, and typography-led hierarchy to create a premium B2B experience that feels like browsing a luxury catalog.</text>

## Approach 2: "Cinematic Contrast" — Greyson Dark Editorial

**Design Movement:** Dark Cinematic Luxury — inspired by Greyson Clothiers' bold dark sections, Tom Ford's product presentation, and the dramatic contrast of Aesop's web presence.

**Core Principles:**
1. Dramatic contrast — Near Black hero sections dominate, punctuated by warm Linen editorial breaks
2. Monumental typography — oversized headlines that command attention
3. Cinematic photography — images treated like film stills, warm golden-hour tones
4. Vertical rhythm — tall sections with generous padding (120px+) create breathing room

**Color Philosophy:** Near Black (#161612) is the dominant canvas, making Forest Green (#243D32) and Gold (#C9A96E) accents pop dramatically. Linen (#F8F3E8) sections feel like intermissions — warm, inviting breaks from the cinematic intensity. This palette says "premium performance brand" not "eco startup."

**Layout Paradigm:** Full-viewport sections stacked vertically. Each section is a "scene." Hero sections are full-bleed with text overlays. Product grids use a masonry-inspired layout with varying card heights. Text sections use centered narrow columns (600px max) for a book-like reading experience. Asymmetric image-text pairs create visual tension.

**Signature Elements:**
1. Oversized bottle count numbers (120px+) in a monospace font that feel architectural
2. Horizontal gold rules that span the full viewport width as scene dividers
3. Subtle grain texture overlay on dark sections for depth and warmth

**Interaction Philosophy:** Cinematic reveals — elements emerge from darkness. Cards have subtle depth on hover with shadow shifts. The persistent CTA button has a gold underline animation on hover. Scroll-linked opacity changes create a sense of depth.

**Animation:** Clip-path reveals for images (wipe from left). Text fades in with slight upward motion. Dark-to-light section transitions use a smooth color morph. Stats/numbers use a typewriter-style count-up. Navigation transitions from transparent to solid on scroll.

**Typography System:** DM Serif Display for hero headlines (48-64px) — editorial authority with warmth. DM Sans for body and subheads. JetBrains Mono for bottle counts and data. Section labels in DM Sans, 13px, ALL-CAPS, wide tracking, Gold.

</idea>
<text>This approach leans into Greyson Clothiers' cinematic darkness, using dramatic Near Black sections with warm Linen intermissions, monumental typography, and film-still photography to create an aspirational B2B brand experience.</text>

## Approach 3: "Quiet Luxury" — Alo Yoga Meets Aesop

**Design Movement:** Quiet Luxury Digital — inspired by Alo Yoga's clean grids, Aesop's intellectual minimalism, and the restrained elegance of Bottega Veneta's digital presence.

**Core Principles:**
1. Radical simplicity — every element earns its place, nothing decorative
2. Material honesty — the product and its story are the decoration
3. Intellectual warmth — copy-forward design where words carry as much weight as images
4. Tactile digital — textures, subtle shadows, and material-inspired surfaces

**Color Philosophy:** Linen (#F8F3E8) and Parchment (#EDE6D6) create a paper-like canvas — as if the website is printed on premium stock. Forest Green (#243D32) is used with extreme restraint — only CTAs and key accents. Gold (#C9A96E) appears only in section labels and thin rules. The palette whispers luxury rather than shouting sustainability.

**Layout Paradigm:** Clean grid with intentional breaks. Two-column layouts where one column is always narrower (text) and the other wider (image). Product pages use a gallery-style grid with equal spacing. Sections are separated by generous whitespace (100px+) rather than dividers. Mobile layout maintains the same proportions through careful responsive scaling.

**Signature Elements:**
1. Thin 1px gold rules that appear and disappear on scroll — like bookmark ribbons
2. Product bottle counts displayed in a refined pill/badge format with Forest Green background
3. Subtle paper texture on the Linen background sections for tactile warmth

**Interaction Philosophy:** Almost invisible. Hover states are color shifts, not movements. Scroll reveals are instant fades (200ms), not dramatic entrances. The website feels like it was always there — you're discovering it, not watching it perform. Focus on content consumption, not interaction spectacle.

**Animation:** Minimal and fast. 200ms fade-in on scroll. No scale transforms. No parallax. Navigation appears/disappears cleanly. Image lazy-loading uses a subtle blur-to-sharp transition. The only "dramatic" animation is the bottle count number reveal — a quick count-up that draws attention to the brand's signature mechanic.

**Typography System:** DM Sans for everything — Bold 700 for headlines (44-56px), Medium 500 for subheads, Regular 400 for body. The monofont consistency creates calm. Space Mono for data/stats only. Section labels in DM Sans Medium, 12px, ALL-CAPS, 0.2em tracking, Gold.

</idea>
<text>This approach channels Alo Yoga and Aesop's intellectual minimalism — radical simplicity where every element earns its place, paper-like textures, and typography-forward design that lets the product story speak without visual noise.</text>
